## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Sales Overview

This page provides a quick summary of overall business performance.

It includes:

- Total Orders, Total Sales, Total Profit, and Total Quantity displayed in numeric form  
- Monthly order trend analysis (March is the highest order-receiving month)  
- Two donut charts showing:
  - Category contribution to total orders  
  - Customer payment mode preferences  
- Top-performing states receiving the highest number of orders  
- Top sellers whose products are most preferred by customers  

This page helps understand priority states, payment trends, category performance, and seller contribution. It gives a complete overview of the business in one place.

![Executive Summary](https://github.com/Tanishkachakole/Amazon-Sales-Dashboard/blob/main/sales_overview.png)

---

### 2. Product Performance

This page focuses on product-level analysis:

- Highest-demand and lowest-demand products (useful for marketing decisions)  
- Product vs Sales analysis to understand revenue contribution  
- Interactive slicer to analyze individual product performance  
- Profit by category to evaluate which categories generate more profit  
- KPI cards displaying:
  - Product Profit Margin  
  - Total Profit  
  - Total Sales  
  - Quantity Sold  
  - Stock Remaining  

This helps identify high-performing and low-performing products and supports inventory and marketing decisions.

![Product Performance](https://github.com/Tanishkachakole/Amazon-Sales-Dashboard/blob/main/product_analysis.png)

---

### 3. Customer Analysis

This page analyzes customer behavior:

- Total Customers by Year  
- New vs Returning Customers  
- Loyal Customers  
- State-wise Customer Distribution  
- Average Order Value (AOV)  

This helps evaluate customer retention, loyalty, and regional performance trends.

![Customer Insights](https://github.com/Tanishkachakole/Amazon-Sales-Dashboard/blob/main/customer_analysis.png)

---

### 4. Trend & Profit Insights

This page provides deeper business analysis:

- Monthly and yearly Sales and Profit trends  
- Day-wise total sales to identify peak sales days  
- Payment mode changes over time  
- Delivery performance comparison  
- Products with highest and lowest stock remaining  

This page is useful to understand demand patterns by month and day and supports better operational decision-making.

![Operational Intelligence](https://github.com/Tanishkachakole/Amazon-Sales-Dashboard/blob/main/trend_analysis.png)

---

## Key Insights

- The business maintained approximately 40% profit margin across the years.  
- Returning customers contribute significantly to total revenue.  
- Certain months show higher demand for lower-priced products, impacting overall profit trends.  
- Payment preferences shift over time.  
- Delivery and return analysis highlight operational improvement opportunities.
